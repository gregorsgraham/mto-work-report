What counts (and what doesn't)
Claimable labour: hours actually working at the site — digging, panning, sampling, marking, mapping, photographing the work. Time spent travelling to and from the site is covered separately under Transportation (per-km, capped at 20%). Time spent writing the report at home is NOT claimable per MTO Info Update #8 — only field hours count.

Realistic volumes: MTO expects volumes to match the labour hours. A solo prospector hand-panning for 4 hours could plausibly test 0.5–1.0 m³ of material. A backhoe in the same 4 hours could move 50+ m³. Pad your "Produced" number with caution — it's usually a tiny fraction of "Tested."

Food / lodging: only claimable for days when you actually worked 6+ hours in the field. Half-days at 4 hrs = 0.5 day on the food line. A travel-only day with no fieldwork doesn't qualify.

Practical filing time: a complete report usually takes 30–90 minutes to fill out in this app once you have your photos, GPS, and cost numbers ready. Not claimable — but the app saves it so the second report goes much faster.

⚠ Important — read before submitting
How to submit your report: Upload through the MTO portal, email to Mineral.Titles@gov.bc.ca with your Event Number in the subject line, or mail/deliver in person to the Vancouver Mineral Titles office, any Service BC office, or Front Counter BC office.

Submit within 30 days of registering your Statement of Work (SOW) — not 30 days after completing the work. (You may see "90 days" elsewhere on MTO — that's the deadline for technical reports, not physical. This app is for physical work, so 30 days applies.)

Incomplete reports will be returned. Make sure all required fields are filled before submitting. If MTO returns yours, you have 14 days to fix and re-submit.

Landowner notification: If your claim overlaps private land or a Crown Land Lease, you must notify the landowner before starting work. This is a separate legal requirement — not part of this report. More info at Mineral Titles →

✅ What counts as physical work
These are the types of work that qualify for assessment credit under the Mineral Tenure Act (Info Update #8):
✓
Placer panning & sluicing — hand panning or sluicing to test for the presence of economically significant minerals
✓
Trenching, open cuts, test pits — hand or mechanical excavation. Mechanical requires a Notice of Work permit.
✓
Reclamation — restoring disturbed ground, replacing topsoil, raking surface material back
✓
GPS survey / ground control survey — precision surveying of the claim area
✓
Line cutting & grid establishment — only when supporting a larger technical work program
✓
Road and trail work — only when supporting a physical work program and with appropriate permit
❌ What does NOT count
✗
Metal detecting — on a placer claim you can use a metal detector but it cannot be counted as assessment work. On a mineral claim it may qualify as prospecting work — contact MTO to confirm before claiming it.
✗
Standalone land clearing — clearing land is only allowed if it supports a larger permitted physical work program
✗
Standalone road work — road and trail work as a standalone activity is not accepted
✗
Mechanical work without a Notice of Work — any mechanized disturbance (excavator, equipment) requires a Mines Act permit first
✗
Work registered more than 1 year after completion — work must be registered within one year of the completion date
