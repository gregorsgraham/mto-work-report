Results & findings Required — different for placer vs mineral
Placer claim
Describe what you found when panning, sluicing, or testing — even if nothing. How many pans/samples, which sites, what material, what results. Evidence of testing is required.
Mineral claim
Describe what your trenching, test pits, or other work found — rock types, mineralization, samples collected, assay results if available. Required if you did any excavation.
Placer — found gold: "Panned 12 samples from creek gravels at Site 1 — found fine colour gold in 4 samples. Estimated 0.1g recovered."
Placer — nothing found: "Panned 20 samples from bench gravels at Sites 1 and 2 — no economically significant minerals recovered. Gravels predominantly clay and decomposed granite."
Mineral — trenching: "Two hand-dug test trenches at Site 1, each 2m long x 0.5m deep. Exposed quartz vein with minor pyrite staining. Rock samples collected for future assay."

Food / Lodging ($50–$100/day · 6 hrs = 1 full day · pro-rated)
Rule (Info Update #8): Each row uses the same formula: hours worked ÷ 6 = fraction of a day × your daily rate.
🏨 Hotel/Motel: You worked (or drove) enough hours that day to justify the stay — enter actual hours, rate $50–$100/night.
⛺ Camping: Same formula, $50/night is the standard rate. Enter the hours you worked that day.
🍽 Meals: Not claimed separately in BC — meals are included in your daily food & lodging rate. Just add a row per worker, enter their hours, and the rate covers meals + accommodation combined.
Example: 4 hrs worked → 4÷6 = 67% of a day → 67% × $50 = $33.33

✅ What counts as physical work
These are the types of work that qualify for assessment credit under the Mineral Tenure Act (Info Update #8):
✓
Placer panning & sluicing — hand panning or sluicing to test for the presence of economically significant minerals
✓
Trenching, open cuts, test pits — hand or mechanical excavation. Mechanical requires a Notice of Work permit.
✓
Reclamation — restoring disturbed ground, replacing topsoil, raking surface material back
✓
GPS survey / ground control survey — precision surveying of the claim area
✓
Line cutting & grid establishment — only when supporting a larger technical work program
✓
Road and trail work — only when supporting a physical work program and with appropriate permit
❌ What does NOT count
✗
Metal detecting — you can use a metal detector on a placer claim but it cannot be counted as assessment work
✗
Standalone land clearing — clearing land is only allowed if it supports a larger permitted physical work program
✗
Standalone road work — road and trail work as a standalone activity is not accepted
✗
Mechanical work without a Notice of Work — any mechanized disturbance (excavator, equipment) requires a Mines Act permit first
✗
Work registered more than 1 year after completion — work must be registered within one year of the completion date
